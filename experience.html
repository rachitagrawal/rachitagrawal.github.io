Exploring Deep Learning Networks for Tumour Segmentation in Infrared Images

Research Intern - Deep Learning, Niramai (Among the 4 Indian start-ups selected For Google's "Launchpad Accelerator"), Dec '17 

Niramai is a health-tech startup that has developed a novel breast cancer screening solution using thermographic images. Given an image, their present algorithm detects the hot spots using a thresholding technique, from which high-level features are further extracted. My work was to explore Deep Learning for the hot spot detection task which is one of semantic segmentation, using very few images. Different classes of models were tried and the best performing model was the V-net with a training dice-coefficient of 0.82, corresponding to a validation dice-coefficient of 0.71. Paper accepted in the Quantitative InfraRed Thermography Conference, 2018 and implementation can be found here.
